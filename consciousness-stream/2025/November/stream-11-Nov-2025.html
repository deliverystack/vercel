JW Consciousness Stream - 11 November 2025

This entry represents something like my stream of consciousness for Tuesday, 11 November, 2025. This is basically like a journal entry that I work on throughout the day and then don't go back to correct errors.

Originally published as: https://deliverystack.net/2025/11/10/jw-consciousness-stream-11-november-2025/

Some songs to start the day:

  • Aplha Blondy: Come Back Jesus: I've been listening to Alpha Blondy since high school. From Côte d'Ivoire, he sings in five languages. I recommend the entire Jerusalem album, and most of the Cocody Rock album is also good. In maybe 2009, I saw Alpha Blondy perform at the Independent Theater in San Francisco. I walked up to the stage and shook his hand while he was performing. "And unity, that's what Jah say."
  • Aplha Blondy: Jerusalem: "You can see Christians, Jews, and Muslims living together and praying, Amen."
  • Alpha Blondy: Super Powers: "In Africa we want Mighty brotherhood."
  • The Mighty Diamonds: I Need a Roof: I saw these guys twice in Berkley (California) around 2009.

This was one of the greatest hip hop shows of my life - more like a party than a show. I really like KRS-ONE in general, but when I saw him a while later, it was just him and a DJ. That show really sucked (partly because the crowd was young and mostly there to see Tabi Bonney, a decent act from DC). I recommend the entire Edutainment album by BDP.
  • Black Star: Astronomy: I saw Talib Kweli a few years ago in Portland, OR, which was a good show. I missed Mos Def in San Francisco about two decades ago, but a friend that went said he mostly lipsynced. Anyway, Black Star is a great hip hop album.

So, today didn't start so well for me. I woke up around 2:00. I looked at my phone to check the time and saw that Kham had sent me some WhatsApp while I was sleeping. Against my better knowledge, I read them. It turns out that her older daughter, Namneung, stole money from me again. This has honestly been a problem since I met them about seven years ago; one of my first memories of Namneung is her taking all of the money from my bag.

In today's case, she had maybe $15USD worth of Lao currency, but she also had a $100US bill, which is a ton of money for a child her age here. Anyway, I was thinking about this, which made it a little difficult to get back to sleep. I got up around 5:00.

I doubt that Namneung knows what the US money is worth. She may in general use money to impress her friends, just as she uses the money and snacks that I give her to help with making and keeping friends. She also "borrows" her mom's phones and apparently sometimes chargers, and then lies, which is a real problem when her mom needs her phone. I think poverty and neglect have led her with a sort of scarcity mindset - she has mimimal educational stimulation, so she steals the phone for entertainment. She has minimal enjoyable food, so she steals snacks for food (she always asks for either candy or snacks that are high in protein, like meat or cheese). Friends are always challenging, so she uses snacks to make friends.

Not being her actual father (her mom tells her that her real father died, which is not true), I'm not sure exactly how to handle this situation. Whatever her mother has been doing for discipline, which definitely involves yelling and apparently involves at least slapping, doesn't seem to have worked. The last time that I saw Namneung torment her mom by not returning the phone, I got upset until she gave it back. I suggested that Kham offer Namneung some kind of allowance that Namneung only gets if she behaves well for the week.

Everybody that sees a picture of my foot calls it ugly or disgusting or something. Actually, my older son called it "Disguusring", but he may have been talking about his spelling rather than my foot. It is somewhat swollen and purplish, possibly worse because I can't seem to keep it elevated, which is hard for example when typing. It also has a sortof Frankensteinish stitches scar and a couple of pins sticking out of the toes, which don't look straight due to those pins, which also keep the whole foot somewhat stiff. Kham gets dizzy looking at my foot, something about metal and blood together. She could't even change the bandage, but the bleeding has stopped so I'm mostly leaving it unbandaged now (except in public).
