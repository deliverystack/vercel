✝️ Where is the Cross?

The prohibition in the Old Testament, the eating of the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil (Genesis 2:17), establishes the principle of judgment: man's autonomy leads to certain death. The tree is an altar of judgment because to eat from it is to substitute man’s judgment for God's, incurring the penalty.

The command in the New Testament to seek the knowledge of Christ (e.g., John 8:32, "You will know the truth, and the truth will set you free," or Philippians 3:10, "that I may know Him") is the principle of mercy. Christ’s work on the Cross is the only legitimate place where the knowledge of good and evil is fully and righteously adjudicated. He is the mediator who provides the substitutionary sacrifice for man’s self-made knowledge. The holiness of God is confronted with the sin of man's self-rule at the Cross, where the death penalty for sin is fully met, and the path to resurrection-life is opened.

👑 What does this say about Jesus?

His Humiliation/ExaltationHis humiliation was standing as the innocent man under the condemnation that came from mankind's stolen knowledge. His exaltation establishes Him as the sole source of true, life-giving knowledge and wisdom from God (1 Corinthians 1:30).

🌳 Sensus Plenior Reading: The Two Types of Knowledge

The shift from the Old Testament prohibition to the New Testament command is not a contradiction, but a progression from the Shadow (the prohibition) to the Substance (Christ).

  1. The Prohibited Knowledge (Autonomous Judgment)

The Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil (Genesis 2:17) was a boundary placed by God's Holiness. The knowledge man sought was the autonomy to judge--to determine for himself what is good and evil, usurping God's judicial throne.

  • Sin: It was not the mere acquisition of a fact, but a posture of self-substitution for God's wisdom. This is the ultimate sin and the origin of death.
  • Judgment and Death-and-Resurrection: The command, "in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die," is the curse of the law. Man was not to gain this knowledge by an act of rebellion, but by obedient maturation under God, who alone possesses perfect wisdom. The Fall brings death, but the promise of the Seed (Genesis 3:15)--Christ--is the resurrection hidden in that death.
  1. The Commanded Knowledge (Revealed Union)

The knowledge Jesus directs man to seek is the knowledge of God revealed through the Son (Matthew 11:27). This knowledge is relational and transformative, not autonomous and intellectual.

  • Water/Word and Light/Holiness: This is the Word/Water of God that now cleanses and illuminates (Light/Holiness). Christ is the Light that dispels the darkness and confusion that came with man’s self-made knowledge.
  • Firmament/Mediation: Jesus is the living Firmament/Boundary/Mediator (Hebrews 10:20, the torn veil/flesh) who allows access to the true Tree of Life (God's presence). He bridges the separation (judgment) between heaven and earth, offering Himself as the path (mercy).
  • Fulfillment: The desire for knowledge is met not by seizing the fruit of judgment, but by receiving the Seed/Word of God (Christ, the resurrection pattern) through faith. Knowing Jesus is the true, sanctified knowledge of good and evil, because we see perfect Goodness (Christ) and perfect Evil (sin) meet and resolve at the Cross.

The difference is ultimately between Knowledge of Self (autonomous, leading to death) and Knowledge of Christ (revealed, leading to life).

The difference between the Old Testament prohibition (Knowledge of Good and Evil) and the New Testament command (Knowledge of Christ) lies in distinguishing between autonomous, self-claimed knowledge and revealed, mediated knowledge through Jesus.

Here is the sensus plenior interpretation distilled into key points:

  • Old Testament Prohibition (Judgment):
    • The Cross: The Tree of Knowledge represents the place of judgment and death. The knowledge sought was the autonomy to judge (self-rule), which is a substitution of man's will for God's, incurring the penalty of death.
    • Holiness vs. Sin: This established God's holiness by setting a boundary against man's sin of usurping divine authority.
  • New Testament Command (Mercy):
    • The Cross & Christ: Jesus, the true Mediator and the new Tree of Life, is the sole source of true knowledge. His Cross is where the judgment for man's self-made knowledge was paid through substitutionary sacrifice.
    • Resurrection: The commanded knowledge is the Revealed Word (Water/Light) of Christ, the Seed who brings resurrection-life from the death incurred by the Fall. Knowing Him moves man from autonomous, death-dealing knowledge to mediated, life-giving knowledge.
