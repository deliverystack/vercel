Summary: 7 Personality Types You Should Never Trust - "Alan Watts"

This article summarizes key points in the video "Watch "7 Personality Types You Should Never Trust - Alan Watts", including a few points of my own. I've seen numerous AI fakes of Alan Watts, so I don't know whether this video uses his voice or even his words. I still found the content valuable because I have dealt with dangerous people that span many of these types and one of my goals in writing is to warn my boys about how to identify risks. Most importantly, you definitely don't want to date such people, be in a committed relationship with them, and especially not marriage.

Originally published to:

  • https://deliverystack.net/2025/12/07/summary-7-personality-types-you-should-never-trust-alan-watts/

Not only should you not trust people with these traits, but you should avoid them altogether, and also warn others about them. Additionally, the worst are those that have multiple characteristics described in this list, which certainly overlap. Individuals may develop these personality characteristics to achieve emotional control over others with more compassion. These people can be very slippery, as they have learned that exposing their true selves too soon will cause others to abandon them. In many cases, entertaining and helping such people simply enables them.

Watts states that you should not avoid these people with judgment, hostility, or contempt but out of wisdom and in your own best interest. He uses a metaphor about a scorpion that stings not because it is evil, but because it is a scorpion. He also notes that we should recognize and avoid these characteristics in ourselves.

  • YouTube: 7 Personality Types You Should Never Trust - "Alan Watts"

The Perpetual Victim

  • Blames others for everything.
  • Does not take responsibility for anything, including their own decisions and actions.
  • May initially treat you as their savior before blaming you for something with which you have no involvement and over which you have no control.
  • Seems almost boastful of their suffering.
  • Wants to consolation, respect, pity, appreciation of their perceived misfortune.
  • They don't actually want solutions, which would threaten their identity.

The Chronic Manipulator

  • Applies emotional pressure.
  • Seems to play games in attempts to achieve various forms of advantage.
  • Makes you feel guilty and pressured.
  • Gets you to do things that you don't want to do.

The Righteous Crusader

  • Always right about everything.
  • Not open to considering outside perspectives.
  • Often judge others and associate only with those with whom they agree.
  • May focus their righteousness on a single subject such as politics, religion, morals, the environment, animal welfare, or otherwise.
  • Anyone that disagrees with their perspective is always wrong.
  • Cling to their beliefs; driven by fear of uncertainty.
  • Relationships are conditional on conformance and agreement with their perspectives.

The Chronic Liar

  • Truth has no value.
  • Life is a performance; they have no true self.
  • Construct their perspective of reality to serve their purposes.
  • Blur reality with illusion to build confidence and increase trust: some of their output is truth with their lies mixed in.

The User

  • Can be pleasant when they get what they want.
  • Always looking for new targets.
  • Friendly when in need; disappear when not or if you decline a request.

The Envious Destroyer

  • Envious of your success; does not want you to be successful.
  • May covertly or overtly sabotage and ruin you.
  • Acts concerned, as if their criticism is constructive.
  • Downplays your successes.

The Chaos Merchant

  • Attracts or creates drama and crises.
  • Pulls others into their predicaments.
  • Chaos is their permanent comfort zone and identity.
  • Makes bad decisions, picks fights and otherwise creates problems.
