General Guidance

This chapter, which I had originally titled The Infinite List because it can grow indefinitely, contains perspetives for general guidance through life. To be clear, I'm not trying to control you or tell me what to do. Following this advice can give you advantages in life.

Beware that intelligence and emotion both have limits and drawbacks. Emotions are generally a signal that you need to strategize and change something in your environment rather than triggering an action.

Before you make any decision or take any relevent risk, consider carefully whether you want children, which is a lifetime commitment. Think about all of the implications, costs, and responsibilities of parenting.

Opportunities Are Everywhere

Every person that you meet and every experience that you have is an opportunity to learn and grow. Those that we consider to be our worst enemies often turn out to be our best teachers. Our greatest challenges can provide some of our most important lessons.

Suffering and struggles develop growth, learning, strength, experience, and hopefully, eventually, wisdom.

Buy vs. Rent

Over time, more and more products and services move towards rental, sharing, and subscription services. In some cases, the convenience is worth the added cost. For example, if you do not drive often, the inconvenience of managing groceries and other purchases through ride hailing services and the hassle and cost of renting cars and trucks when needed may be more cost-effective than buying, insuring, and maintaining a car. In other circumstances and for other products and services, ownership can have financial and other advantages. If you will watch a movie several times, it is better to buy it than to rent it.

What Can They Offer

Before committing to another person, determine what they have to offer. While people often prefer to believe that relationships are not transactional, in truth they often are. A person works for their boss because they receive money in exchange for their efforts. A committed intimate relationship can only work of both parties contribute in equal measure - not necessarily with the same goods and services, but with equivalent effort. Exit relationships in which the other party does not offer approximately what you will. There are always other people that you can befriend and with whom you can enter intimate relationships. Be patient and choose the best partners in both business and personal relationships, especially in starting a company or committing to monogamous intimacy for the long term.

Ladders Are Dangerous

Ladders may be the most dangerous tool that you will ever use. Follow ladder safety rules such as:

  • Selecting the appropriate ladder for the task.
  • Check ladder condition before use.
  • Ensure that the ladder user has the required physical capabilities.
  • Configure the ladder to be level and safe.
  • Move the ladder when appropriate and beware of its surroundings in each new position.
  • Never lean or reach far from the ladder.
  • Never take more than one limb (one hand or one foot) from the ladder. In general, avoid carrying tools and materials up or down the ladder. Have someone hand them to you when you are in the right position or use a rope or other conveyance.
  • Only take small steps up and down the ladder.
  • When leaning a ladder, position the base of the ladder at a distance from the structure of one fourth the height of the point of contact between the ladder and the structure.
  • Climb down slowly and cautiously, facing the ladder.

Kitchens and Bathrooms Are Dangerous

While some of this may be most relevent to elderly and disabled persons, it applies to everyone. Kitchens and bathrooms are the most dangerous rooms in the house, kitchens due to fires and bathrooms due to slips. Check for an appropriate number of functional smoke and carbon monoxide detection systems monthly. Change all batteries before they expire. Ensure bathrooms have stabilization bars and textured floor surfaces.

The Four Agreements

The Four Agreements: A Practical Guide to Personal Freedom is a self-help book by the author Don Miguel Ruiz. The book outlines a code of conduct, supposedly based on Toltec teachings that purport to improve one's life.

  • Wikipedia: The Four Agreements

These are basically four suggested commitments to make with yourself that the author believes result in greater life satisfaction.

  1. Be impeccable with your word.
  2. Don't make assumptions. For example, when something is missing, don't assume that someone stole it. More than likely, it's where you left it, and you simply misplaced it or forgot where you put it. This is especially true when you try to hide something from others and then forget that you hid it or where you last hid it.
  3. Don't take things personally.
  4. Do your best.
